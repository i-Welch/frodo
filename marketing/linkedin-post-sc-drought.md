South Carolina chartered 56 banks in the 15 years before 2010.

In the 16 years since: one.

That one bank, Beacon Community Bank, opened in January 2018 and grew from $36 million in assets to $972 million. Demand was never the problem.

Meanwhile 13 South Carolina charters have been retired since 2018, almost all through acquisition, and the state is down to 44 FDIC-insured institutions. The Census Bureau ranked South Carolina the fastest-growing state in America in 2023. Florida has filed 5 of the nation's 15 bank applications this year. Alabama chartered two banks in two years. South Carolina has nothing pending.

I pulled the full FDIC data on the drought: why the door closed in 2010, what happened to the class of 56, and what it would take for an organizing group to break the streak.
